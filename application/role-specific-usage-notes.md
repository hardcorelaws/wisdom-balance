# Role-Specific Usage Notes

The Wisdom Balance may be used by different roles in different ways. The core model remains the same: leaders examine visible pressures, look beneath them into the deeper forces of People, Decisions, Shared Values, and Controls, and use prudent responses to restore equilibrium.

These usage notes are intended to help different professional audiences approach the framework from their own responsibilities. They do not change the model. They provide practical entry points for its use.

## Important Note

These questions are intended to provoke thinking, reflection, and discussion. They are not an exhaustive checklist, nor should they be applied mechanically.

Some questions may not apply to a specific initiative. Other questions may need to be added depending on the initiative’s nature, context, risk, complexity, and visible pressure.

The practical task is to begin with the visible pressure, examine the relevant forces of People, Decisions, Shared Values, and Controls, and identify what must be balanced or rebalanced to restore equilibrium in the system.

## For Executive Sponsors

Executive sponsors can use The Wisdom Balance to look beyond status reporting and examine whether the initiative remains aligned with purpose, value, and organizational intent.

Sponsors often see the visible pressures first: delays, cost concerns, escalations, stakeholder dissatisfaction, or unclear benefits. The Wisdom Balance helps sponsors ask whether these pressures are symptoms of deeper imbalance.

**Useful questions for sponsors:**

- Is the initiative still serving the strategic purpose that justified its existence?
- What assumptions about value, risk, or organisational readiness have changed since approval?
- Are we solving the problem we originally intended to solve, or has the initiative drifted from its purpose?
- Which stakeholder interests are shaping decisions, and are they aligned with the organisation’s long-term interests?
- What important truths might not be reaching the governance forum?
- Are leaders making difficult decisions, or are they managing perceptions while avoiding them?
- What behaviours are being rewarded under current pressures, and do they reflect our stated values?
- Are governance controls helping us understand reality, or creating confidence without insight?
- If this initiative were proposed today, knowing what we now know, would we still choose to invest in it?
- What value must be protected even if scope, schedule, budget, or expectations must change?
- What decision have we delayed because it is politically difficult, organisationally uncomfortable, or personally inconvenient?
- What consequence are we accepting by choosing not to act now?

Sponsors should use the model to strengthen clarity, remove ambiguity, protect value, and ensure that governance conversations do not become ritual.

## For PMO Leaders

PMO leaders can use The Wisdom Balance to improve governance conversations, recovery reviews, portfolio discussions, and lessons learned.

The model helps the PMO move beyond reporting mechanics to examine the quality of leadership, decision-making, stakeholder alignment, governance discipline, and organizational behavior surrounding initiatives.

**Useful questions for PMO leaders:**

- Are governance controls helping leaders see reality?
- Are repeated issues symptoms of a deeper imbalance?
- Are people compensating for weak decisions or weak controls?
- Is the PMO enabling judgment or producing administrative theatre?
- Where are initiatives repeatedly losing equilibrium?
- What patterns are visible across multiple initiatives?

PMO leaders should use the model to raise the quality of professional conversation. Its purpose is to improve insight, not to add another reporting layer.

## For Project and Program Managers

Project and program managers can use The Wisdom Balance as a diagnostic lens during planning, execution, escalation, recovery, and phase transitions.

The framework helps them distinguish between visible delivery pressure and the deeper forces that may be creating it. A schedule delay may not be only a planning issue. A cost concern may not be only a budget issue. A quality issue may not be only a technical issue.

**Useful questions for project and program managers:**

- What visible pressure is appearing?
- Which deeper force may be driving it?
- Are people aligned, tired, cautious, resistant, or silently compensating?
- What decision has been delayed, avoided, or poorly understood?
- What value is being tested under pressure?
- Are controls proportionate to the initiative’s complexity and risk?
- What wise action would restore balance without creating more serious damage?

Project and program managers should use the model to diagnose earlier, escalate more intelligently, and intervene with better judgment.

## For Delivery Teams

Delivery teams can use The Wisdom Balance to understand the conditions affecting their work and to surface concerns before they become failure points.

The model can help teams discuss confusion, unclear decisions, weak communication, shifting expectations, excessive controls, poor morale, or misalignment without reducing every issue to personal blame.

**Useful questions for delivery teams:**

- Do we understand the purpose of the initiative?
- Are we clear about decisions, priorities, and expectations?
- Are we able to speak honestly about risks and concerns?
- Are controls helping us deliver or slowing meaningful progress?
- Are we compensating for unresolved problems elsewhere?
- What condition is making the work harder than it needs to be?

Teams should use the model to create clarity, improve communication, and make hidden friction visible in a constructive manner.

## For Governance Bodies

Governance bodies can use The Wisdom Balance to assess whether oversight is creating useful visibility, timely decisions, and appropriate corrective action.

Governance often fails when it becomes a review ritual. The Wisdom Balance helps governance bodies ask whether they are seeing the initiative’s true condition or only its formal presentation.

**Useful questions for governance bodies:**

- Are reports revealing reality or preserving appearances?
- Are risks and issues owned, understood, and acted upon?
- Are escalation paths working?
- Are decisions being made with sufficient context and authority?
- Are controls proportionate to the initiative’s risk and complexity?
- Is governance restraining chaos or concealing it?

Governance bodies should use the model to improve oversight and decision-making quality. The goal is not more governance. The goal is wiser governance.

## For Consultants and Advisors

Consultants and advisors can use The Wisdom Balance as a structured conversation tool to diagnose initiative conditions, facilitate executive dialogue, and support recovery or realignment work.

The model is useful when visible symptoms are known, but the deeper causes remain unclear. It can help structure conversations without forcing premature conclusions.

**Useful questions for consultants and advisors:**

- What pressure is the client reporting most often?
- What explanation is being repeated?
- What explanation is being avoided?
- Which of the four balancing forces is weakest, excessive, neglected, or distorted?
- Is the organization treating symptoms while avoiding the underlying imbalance?
- What intervention would restore equilibrium without increasing dependency?

Consultants and advisors should use the model with restraint. It should help the client see the system more clearly, not create dependency on the consultant’s interpretation.

## For Coaches and Change Practitioners

Coaches and change practitioners can use The Wisdom Balance to examine the human, decision, value, and control conditions that shape adaptability.

The framework is especially useful when teams are expected to adapt but the wider environment continues to create ambiguity, decision delays, conflicting incentives, or governance tensions.

**Useful questions for coaches and change practitioners:**

- Is the initiative adapting with purpose or reacting without direction?
- Are decision rights clear enough to support timely learning?
- Are teams empowered in practice or only in language?
- Are shared values influencing behavior under pressure?
- Are controls enabling learning or enforcing ritual compliance?
- What change is the system resisting, and why?

Coaches and change practitioners should use the model to connect adaptability with leadership judgment, governance maturity, and organizational behavior.

## For Risk, Audit, and Assurance Professionals

Risk, audit, and assurance professionals can use The Wisdom Balance to assess whether the initiative's deeper conditions support reliable control, ethical conduct, and truthful reporting.

The model helps assurance work move beyond compliance evidence and examine whether controls are proportionate, risks are understood, and leadership is receiving accurate signals.

**Useful questions for risk, audit, and assurance professionals:**

- Are controls operating as intended?
- Are controls revealing reality or creating defensive behavior?
- Are risks and issues being surfaced early enough?
- Are decisions supported by reliable information?
- Are values being compromised under delivery pressure?
- Where might formal compliance conceal practical weakness?

Risk, audit, and assurance professionals should use the model to strengthen judgment about control effectiveness, not merely to confirm the existence of controls.

## For Leadership Teams

Leadership teams can use The Wisdom Balance during strategic reviews, initiative recovery, transformation oversight, and difficult alignment conversations.

The model helps leadership teams examine how their own behavior, decisions, values, and governance practices influence the health of an initiative.

**Useful questions for leadership teams:**

- Are we aligned on the purpose of this initiative?
- Are we creating clarity or adding ambiguity?
- Are we making decisions that protect long-term value?
- Are our stated values visible in our conduct under pressure?
- Are our controls giving us truth or comfort?
- What are we tolerating because it is inconvenient to confront?

Leadership teams should use the model for honest reflection. Its value increases when leaders are willing to examine their own contribution to imbalance.

## For Educators and Trainers

Educators and trainers can use The Wisdom Balance as a teaching framework for leadership judgment in complex initiatives.

The model can help learners understand that initiative success is shaped by more than process, method, tools, or technical skill. It introduces the need to examine visible pressure, deeper forces, and wise response.

**Useful teaching applications:**

- Case discussion
- Leadership reflection
- Governance simulation
- Project recovery analysis
- Stakeholder alignment exercises
- Lessons learned workshops
- Executive education discussions

**Useful questions for educators and trainers:**

- What visible pressure appears in the case?
- Which deeper force is most relevant?
- What is the leadership judgment required?
- What response would restore balance?
- What misuse of the model should be avoided?

Educators and trainers should present the model as a leadership lens, not as a mechanical checklist.

## For Individual Leaders

Individual leaders can use The Wisdom Balance as a reflective tool for their own conduct, judgment, and decision-making.

The model helps leaders pause before reacting to pressure. It encourages them to ask whether the issue before them is truly the issue or a symptom of imbalance elsewhere in the system.

**Useful questions for individual leaders:**

- What am I seeing?
- What am I avoiding?
- What pressure is visible?
- What force may be out of balance?
- What decision is now required?
- What value must I protect?
- What action would restore balance with the least unnecessary damage?

Individual leaders should use the model to improve judgment, not to create certainty. The model is most useful when it helps a leader see more clearly and act more prudently.

## Common Guidance Across Roles

Regardless of role, The Wisdom Balance should be used with seriousness, candor, and proportion.

**Use it to:**

- Ask better questions
- Look beneath visible symptoms
- Examine the deeper forces shaping the initiative movement
- Improve leadership judgment
-  Strengthen governance conversations
- Restore alignment and purposeful progress

**Do not use it to:**

- Create another administrative ritual
- Replace established delivery methods
- Avoid accountability
- Suppress dissent
- Justify personal preference as wisdom
- Reduce qualitative judgment to false precision
- Decorate reports without changing behavior

The Wisdom Balance is most valuable when it helps leaders see what the system has been trying to show them.
